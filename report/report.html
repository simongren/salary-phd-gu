PhD Salary Development at Göteborgs Universitet, 2022–2025

⚠ Fully AI-generated. This report and all visualisations were generated by GitHub Copilot (Claude Sonnet 4.5) based on the official negotiated salary agreements published by Göteborgs Universitet. No manual analysis has been performed. The purpose is purely to share structured information about salary development over time.

Background

Each autumn, PhD salaries at GU are renegotiated locally at the faculty level. The agreements set four salary steps tied to the share of completed PhD requirements:

StepMeaning
0 %Entry level — newly admitted
50 %Mid-way through doctoral studies
80 %Near completion
100 %Post-defence / doctoral degree awarded

This report covers agreements effective 1 October in 2022, 2023, 2024 and 2025, across all GU faculties. The dataset contains 48 observations (12 PhD groups × 4 years).

Salary at 80% completion — all groups, 2022–2025

Monthly salary (SEK) at 80% completion per PhD group, broken out by faculty. Labels show group identity at the 2025 data point. Y-axis is free-scaled per panel.

This chart shows the absolute monthly salary at the 80% completion step — the most commonly cited benchmark — for every PhD group. The chart is split into four panels, one per faculty grouping. Each line is one PhD group.

This gives a straightforward picture of where salary levels sit and how they have moved in krona terms year-on-year. Sahlgrenska has both the highest salaries and the most groups; the Humanities/Social Sciences/Arts/Business grouping and the Education faculty have the lowest absolute levels.

Indexed salary vs. Swedish CPIF inflation (80% level)

All salaries re-indexed so that 2022 = 100. The dashed red line is cumulative Swedish CPIF (Oct-to-Oct). Lines above the dashed line indicate growth above inflation; lines below indicate real-terms erosion.

Re-expressing salaries as an index (2022 = 100) removes the effect of different starting levels and makes it easy to compare growth rates across very different groups. The dashed red reference line shows cumulative CPIF inflation over the same period, measured October-to-October to match the timing of the salary negotiations.

Data & Code

All source PDFs, the extracted dataset and the R visualisation script are openly available at:
github.com/simongren/salary-phd-gu

Data was extracted from the official PDF attachments (Bilaga 1) to the locally negotiated salary agreements (Lokalt avtal om lönessättningsprinciper för doktorander, Dnr P 2016/121 and subsequent years). The 2025 agreement PDF was image-based and required OCR processing (Tesseract, Swedish language model).
